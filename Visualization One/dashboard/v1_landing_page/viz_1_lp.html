Analyzing Weather Patterns with Respect to Latitude




Goals



    Methodology



                  Insights


      The aim of these visualizations is to display the
      effect location has on various weather metrics.

      For our purposes, I have examined latitude's
      effect on temperature (in farenheit), humidity,
      wind speed (in miles per hour), and cloudiness.

      If you would like to see the raw data, you may
      find it here.

500 cities were randomly selected (the cities were
randomized based on two randomly selected values
via numpy accounting for longitude and latitude.)
The city was then determined using the citipy
library.

The weather observations used were from that
same moment on November 11th, 2018.
Regions with missing measurements were filtered
from the list. The data was pulled from the
OpenWeatherMap API.

Sidenote: Thank you OWM for the excellent resource!

The first graph that we have is of the temperature
versus the latitude. It appears we have a nice
correlation.

The second graph is of the humidity versus the latitude.
There is little to say about this one.

The third graph is of the windspeed versus the latitude.
Again there is little to say here.

The fourth and last graph is of the cloudiness versus
the latitude. There appears to be almost no correlation
here.
